reading list

Week 1: Overview

  • [Optional] Copley, B. & F. Martin. Introduction to Causation in Grammatical Structures. [Library access]

  • [Optional] Menzies, P. Counterfactual theories of causation. In E. Zalta (ed.), The Stanford Encyclopedia of Philosophy, Metaphysics Research Lab, Stanford University winter 2017 edn.

Week 2: CAUSE, lexical decomposition

  • Dowty, D. 1979. Word Meaning and Montague Grammar. Dordrecht: Reidel. [Chapter 2]
    • Section 2.1 (background); 2.3. Section 2.2 recommended if you are not familiar with lexical aspect.

  • Fodor, J. 1970. Three reasons for not deriving "kill" from "cause to die". Linguistic Inquiry 1, 429-438.

  • McCawley, J. 1978. Conversational implicature and the lexicon. In P. Cole (ed.), Syntax and Semantics 9: Pragmatics, 245–258. New York: Routledge.

Notes on this week's readings (handwritten).


Week 3: Typology

  • Comrie, B. 1989. Language Universals and Linguistic Typology. Chicago: University of Chicago Press. Chapter 8.

  • Shibatani, M. 1976. Causativization. In Shibatani, M (ed.), Syntax and Semantics 5: Japanese generative grammar, 239-294. New York: Academic Press.

  • [Optional] Bhatt, R. 2003. Causativization. Handout from "Topics in the Syntax of the Modern Indo-Aryan Languages", on ways of causative formation.
    • Look over to get a better sense of patterns of causativization in this language group


Weeks 4-5: Direct and indirect causation

  • Wolff, P. 2003. Direct causation in the linguistic coding and individuation of causal events. Cognition 88: 1-48.

  • Neeleman, A. & H. van de Koot. 2012. The linguistic expression of causation. In M. Everaert, M. Marelj, & T. Siloni (eds.), The Theta System: Argument Structure at the Interface. Oxford: Oxford University Press.


Week 6: The causative alternation

  • Levin, B. 2015. Semantics and pragmatics of argument alternations. Annual Review of Linguistics 1, 68-83. Sections 1, 2, and 5.

  • Levin, B & M. Rappaport Hovav. 1994. A preliminary analysis of causative verbs in English. Lingua 92: 35-77.

  • [Optional, overview] Schäfer, F. 2009. The causative alternation. Language & Linguistics Compass 3, 641--681.


Week 7: Internal and external causation

  • McKoon, G. & T. MacFarland. 2000. Externally and internally caused change of state verbs. Language 76, 833–858.

  • Wright, S. 2002. Transitivity and change of state verbs. In Proceedings of the Berkeley Linguistics Society, 339–350.

Week 8: Agentive causation

  • DeLancey, S. 1984. Notes on agentivity and causation. Studies in Language 8, 181-213.

  • [Optional] Kittilä, S. 2005. Remarks on involuntary agent constructions. Word 56, 381-419.

Week 9: Event structure

  • Davidson, D. 1969. The individuation of events. In N. Rescher, ed., Essays in Honor of Carl G. Hempel. Dordrecht: Springer.

  • Croft, W. 1991. Syntactic Categories and Grammatical Relations. Chicago: University of Chicago Press. [Sections 4.4, 5.2 (pp.159-181, 184-192)]

  • [Optional background] Davidson, D. 1967 The logical form of action sentences. In N. Rescher, ed., The Logic of Decision and Action. Pittsburg: University of Pittsburgh Press.

Week 10: Alternative approaches to causation

  • Lewis, D. 1973. Causation Journal of Philosophy 70, 556-567.

  • Copley, B. & P. Wolff. 2014. Theories of causation should inform linguistic theory and vice versa. In B. Copley & F. Martin, eds., Causation in Grammatical Structures, 11-57. Oxford: Oxford University Press.


Week 11: Structural causal models

  • Sloman, S. 2005. Causal Models: How People Think About the World and Its Alternatives. Oxford: Oxford University Press. [Chapters 3-5]


Week 12: Causal models continued

  • Lauer, S. & P. Nadathur. 2019, accepted. Causal necessity, causal sufficiency, and the implications of causative verbs. Glossa.

  • Martin, F. 2018. Time in probabilistic causation: direct vs. indirect uses of lexical causative verbs. In Proceedings of Sinn und Bedeutung 22.


Week 13: Force dynamics

  • Wolff, P. 2014. Causal pluralism and force dynamics. In B. Copley & F. Martin, eds., Causation in Grammatical Structures, 100-119. Oxford: Oxford University Press.

  • Copley, B. & H. Harley. 2014. Eliminating causative entailments with the force-theoretic framework. In B. Copley & F. Martin, eds., Causation in Grammatical Structures, 120-151. Oxford: Oxford University Press.


Week 14: Causation and modality

  • Hobbs, J. 2003. Causality and modality: the case of 'would'. Journal of Semantics 20.



Week 15: Implicatives

  • Baglini, R. & I. Francez. 2016. The implications of managing. Journal of Semantics 33, 541-560.
