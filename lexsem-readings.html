reading list

Week 1: Overview

  • [Optional] Barker, C. 2006. Lexical semantics. Encyclopedia of Cognitive Science, 1-7.

  • [Optional] Grice, H.P. 1957. Meaning. The Philosophical Review 66, 377-388.

Week 2: Polysemy
Sample solutions for Assignment 1.

  • Cruse, D.A. 1995. Polysemy and related phenomena from a cognitive linguistic viewpoint. In P. Saint-Dizier and E. Viegas, eds., Computational Lexical Semantics, 33-49. Cambridge: Cambridge University Press.

  • Dölling J. To appear. Systematic polysemy. In L. Matthewson, C. Meier, H. Rullmann, and T. E. Zimmermann, eds., The Blackwell Companion to Semantics.

  • [Useful supplementary readings] Special issue on current approaches to polysemy, Lingua 2015. Recommended:
    • Falkum, I. & A. Vicente [Editorial overview.] Polysemy: current perspectives and approaches.
    • Frisson, S. About bound and scary books: the processing of book polysemies.
    • Vicente, A. The green leaves and the expert: polysemy and truth-conditional variability.
    • Asher, N. Types, meanings and coercions in lexical semantics.
      NB: Probably a bit harder to read, but worthwhile if you're interested in lexical semantics (and especially the mass/count distinction in nouns or the lexical/grammatical aspect interface.
    • Srinivasan, M. & H. Rabagliati. How concepts and conventions structure the lexicon: cross-linguistic evidence from polysemy.

  • [Not required, possibly helpful] Cruse, D.A. 2004. Lexical facets and metonymy. Ilha do Desterro 47, 73--96.

My reading notes on Cruse and Dölling. Handwritten, may be hard to read. No promises!


Week 3: Lexical encoding

  • [Optional, recommended] Fillmore, C. 1969. Types of lexical information. In F. Kiefer, ed., Studies in Syntax and Semantics. Foundations of Language (Supplementary Series), vol 10. Dordrecht: Springer.

  • Fillmore, C. 1971. Verbs of judging: an exercise in semantic description. In C. Fillmore and T. Langendoen, eds., Studies in Linguistic Semantics, 272-289. New York: Holt, Reinhart and Winston, Inc.

  • McCawley, J. 1979. Verbs of bitching. In J. McCawley, Adverbs, Vowels, and Other Objects of Wonder, 135-150. Chicago: University of Chicago Press..

My reading notes.


Week 4: Lexical information across categories

  • Clark, E. & H. Clark. 1979. When nouns surface as verbs. Language 5, 767-811.

  • Kiparsky, P. 1997. Remarks on denominal verbs. In A. Alsina, J. Bresnan & P. Sells, eds., Complex Predicates, 473-499. Stanford: CSLI Publications.


Week 5: Nouns and countability

  • Murphy, M.L. 2010. Lexical Meaning. Cambridge: Cambridge University Press. Chapter 8.


Week 6: Adjectives

  • Murphy, M.L. 2010. Lexical Meaning. Cambridge: Cambridge University Press. Chapter 11.

  • Dixon, R.M.W. 1982. Where have all the adjectives gone? In Where Have All the Adjectives Gone? and other essays in Semantics and Syntax. Berlin: Mouton.

  • Kennedy, C. 2012. Adjectives. In G. Russell & D.G. Fara (eds.), Routledge Companion to Philosophy of Language. Routledge.

  • [Optional] Wierzbicka, A. 1988. What's in a noun? In The Semantics of Grammar. Amsterdam: John Benjamins.


Week 7: Scales and pragmatic enrichment

  • Horn, L. 1984. Toward a new taxonomy for pragmatic inference: Q-based and R-based implicature. In D. Schiffrin, ed., Meaning, Form, and Use in Context: Linguistic Applications, 11-42. Washington, D.C.: Georgetown University Press.


Week 8: Thematic roles

  • Davis, A.R. 2011. Thematic roles. In C. Maienborn, K. von Heusinger, & P. Portner (eds.), Semantics: An International Handbook of Natural Language Meaning. 399-420. Berlin: Mouton de Gruyter.

  • Levin, B. & M. Rappaport Hovav. 2005. Argument Realization. Cambridge: Cambridge University Press. [Chapter 2, all but section 2.3; Chapter 3 from beginning up to 3.1.2]


Week 9: Argument structure and alternations

  • Johnson, K. 2008. An Overview of Lexical Semantics. Philosophy Compass 3: 119-134.

  • Fillmore, C. 1970. The grammar of hitting and breaking. In R. Jacobs and P. Rosenbaum, eds., Readings in English Transformational Grammar, 120-133. Waltham: Ginn.


Week 10: Analytic causatives

  • Wierzbicka, A. 1998. The semantics of English causative constructions in a universal-typological perspective. In M. Tomasello, ed., The New Psychology of Language: Cognitive and Functional Approaches to Language Structure, 113-153. Mahwah, NJ: Lawrence Erlbaum.
