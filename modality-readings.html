reading list

Week 2: Formal basics

  • von Fintel, K. & I. Heim. 2011. Intensional semantics: lecture notes. Ms, MIT. [Chapters 1-2]

  • Kaufmann, S., C. Condoravdi & V. Harizanov. 2006. Formal approaches to modality. In The Expression of Modality, W. Frawley (ed.). Berlin: De Gruyter.


Week 3: Premise semantics (overview)

  • Portner, P. 2009. Modality. Oxford: Blackwell. [Chapters 1-2]

  • von Fintel, K. & I. Heim. 2011. Intensional semantics: lecture notes.. Ms, MIT. [Chapter 3]


Week 4: Premise semantics

  • Lewis, D. 1975. Adverbs of quantification. In Formal Semantics: The Essential Readings, P. Portner & B. Partee (eds.)

  • Kratzer, A. 1981. The notional category of modality. In Words, Worlds, and Contexts: New approaches to word semantics, H.-J. Eikmeyer & H. Rieser (eds.), 38-74. Berlin: De Gruyter.

  • Kratzer, A. 1991. Modality. In Semantics: An International Handbook of Contemporary Research, A. von Stechow & D. Wunderlich (eds.), 639-650. Berlin: De Gruyter.


Week 5: Epistemic modals

  • von Fintel, K. & A. Gillies. 2007. An opinionated guide to epistemic modality. In Oxford Studies in Epistemology, Vol. 2, T. Gendler & J. Hawthorne (eds), 32-62. Oxford: Oxford University Press.

  • von Fintel, K. & A. Gillies. 2008. CIA leaks. The Philosophical Review 117: 77-98.


Week 6: Attitudes and evidentials

  • Stephenson, T. 2007. Judge dependence, epistemic modals, & predicates of personal taste. Linguistics & Philosophy 30: 487-525.

  • Anand, P. & V. Hacquard. 2008. Epistemics with attitude. Proceedings of Semantics & Linguistic Theory 18.


Week 7: Modal force

  • von Fintel, K. & S. Iatridou. 2008. How to say 'ought' in foreign: the compositional of weak necessity modals. In Time and Modality, J. Guéron & J. Lecarme (eds), 115-141. Springer.

  • Deal, A.R. 2011. Modals without scales. Language 87: 559-585.

  • Yanovich, I. 2016. Old English *motan, variable-force modality, and the presupposition of inevitable actualization. Language 92: 489-521.


Week 8: Deontic modals

  • Portner, P. 2009. Modality. Oxford: Blackwell. [Chapter 4]

  • von Fintel, K. & I. Heim. 2011. Intensional semantics: lecture notes.. Ms, MIT. [Chapter 5]

  • von Fintel, K. 2012. The best we can (expect to) get? Challenges to the classic semantics for deontic modals. Ms, MIT.


Week 9: Ability modals

  • Brown, M. 1988. On the logic of ability. Journal of Philosophical Logic 17: 1-26.

  • Mandelkern, M., G. Schultheis, & D. Boylan. 2017.

  • Nadathur, P. 2019. Causality, aspect, and modality in actuality inferences. Ph.D. dissertation, Stanford University. [Chapters 1-2]

  • Hacquard, V. 2016. Actuality entailments. In The Companion to Semantics, D. Gutzmann, L. Matthewson, C. Meier, H. Rullman, & T.E. Zimmerman (eds). Oxford: Wiley.


Week 10: Conditionals (overview)

  • von Fintel, K. & I. Heim. 2011. Intensional semantics: lecture notes.. Ms, MIT. [Chapter 4]

  • Kratzer, A. 1986. Conditionals. In Proceedings of the Chicago Linguistic Society 22: 1-15.

  • von Fintel, K. & S. Iatridou. 2002. If and when if-clauses can restrict quantifiers. Workshop in Philosophy & Language at the University of Michigan. November 8-10.


Week 11: Counterfactuals

  • Stalnaker, R. 1968. A theory of conditionals. Studies in Logical Theory 2: 98-112.

  • Lewis, D. 1973. Counterfactuals and comparative possibility.Journal of Philosophical Logic 2: 418-446.

  • Lewis, D. 1981. Ordering semantics and the premise semantics for counterfactuals. Journal of Philosophical Logic 10: 217-234.


Week 12: Indicative and subjunctive conditionals

  • Grice, H.P. 1967. Indicative conditionals. In Studies in the Way of Words 58-85. Cambridge, MA: Harvard University Press.

  • Stalnaker, R. 1975. Indicative conditionals. Philosophia 5: 269-286.

  • Leahy, B. 2011. Presuppositions and antipresuppositions in conditionals. In Semantics & Linguistic Theory 21: 257-274.

  • Heim, I. 1992. Presupposition projection and the semantics of attitude verbs. Journal of Semantics 9: 183-221.

  • Ippolito, M. 2006. Semantic composition and presupposition projection in subjunctive conditionals. Linguistics & Philosophy 29: 631-672.


Week 13: Pragmatic interpretations of conditionals

  • Geis, M. & A. Zwicky. 1971. On invited inferences. Linguistic Inquiry 2: 561-566.

  • van der Auwera, J. 1997. Pragmatics in the last quarter century: the case of conditional perfection. Journal of Pragmatics 27: 261-274.

  • Horn, L. 2000. From if to iff: conditional perfection as pragmatic strengthening. Journal of Pragmatics 32: 289-326.

  • von Fintel, K. 2001. Conditional strengthening: a case study in implicature. Ms, MIT.

  • Nadathur, P. 2013. If ... (and only if): conditional perfection and completeness. M.Phil. thesis, University of Oxford. [Chapters 3-4]

  • Franke, M. 2007. The pragmatics of biscuit conditionals. Proceedings of the 16th Amsterdam Colloquium, M. Aloni, P. Dekker, & F. Roelofsen (eds), 91-96.

  • Moss, S. 2012. On the pragmatics of counterfactuals. Noûs 46: 561-586.
