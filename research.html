current and recent projects

  • Dissertation: Causality, aspect, and modality in actuality inferences. Stanford Unversity, 2019.
  • Causal necessity, causal sufficiency, and the implications of causative verbs -- with Sven Lauer. An informal presentation of a causal-modeling approach to the lexical semantics of periphrastic causatives; manuscript under review, comments welcome.
  • Sufficiency causatives -- with Sven Lauer A more technical presentation of sufficiency in causative verbs, in revision.
  • Aspect, abilities, dispositions, and actuality entailments. Recent paper: Implicative inferences and causality in enough and too constructions.
  • Exceptive phrases and conditionals (with Dan Lassiter). Recent paper: Unless, exceptives, and the pragmatics of conditionals.

manuscripts

  • 2016. Language, perception, and thought: what can neo-Whorfianism teach us about cognition? Final for course on the foundations of linguistic theory: a (fairly broad) survey of experimental work on the connection between language and cognition, that builds on Frank, et al (2008)'s notion of language as a "cognitive technology."
  • 2016. Focus improvements to weak crossover. Second qualifying paper, Stanford.
    Comments: This is a very incomplete piece of work in many, if not all, respects. I remain very interested in the issues that working on this project raised (the semantic analysis of questions and focus, processing accounts of grammaticality judgements, the differences and overlap between emphatic focus and focus particles), and hope to return to these questions at some point when I am better equipped to tackle at least one of them).

  • 2007. An introduction to homology. Manuscript, University of Chicago Mathematics REU.
    Comments: Unexpectedly, around 2017, people started to email me infrequently, but regularly, about this paper. This was written at the end of the 2007 UChicago math REU program, when I still had the mistaken belief that I was going to be a mathematician and was clumsily trying to teach myself some algebraic topology (and, incidentally, LaTeX). Nothing in this manuscript is original, other than the particular way I went about trying to clarify concepts to myself, and -- unfortunately -- at this point, it would take much more time and effort than it is worth to get myself back to a state where I could easily read and summarize its contents. I'm glad it seems to have been helpful to strangers on the internet, and I'm very sorry I'm now unable to answer any of their questions about it.
